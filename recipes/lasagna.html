Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian comfort food made by layering pasta sheets with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. Baked to golden perfection, it's hearty, flavorful, and perfect for family dinners.

This dish is versatile too- you can make it with beef, pork, or even vegetables for a lighter twist. No matter the version, lasagna is always a warm and satisfying favorite.

Ingredients:

  • Lasagna sheets
  • Ground beef or sausage
  • Tomato sauce
  • Onion and garlic
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh basil or parsley

Cooking steps

    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Cook the lasagna sheets according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
    3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
    4. Add ground beef (or sausage) to the skillet and cook until browned. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of fresh herbs.
    5. Pour in tomato sauce and simmer for 10-15 minutes to let the flavors combine.
    6. In a separate bowl, mix ricotta cheese with a bit of salt, pepper, and some chopped parsley or basil.
    7. In a baking dish, start layering: spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom, then a layer of lasagna sheets, followed by ricotta mixture, and sprinkle mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with cheese on top.
    8. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
    9. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
